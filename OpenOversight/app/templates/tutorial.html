OpenOversight Labeling Tutorial

We want you to help us go through images submitted by activists and Lucy Parsons Labs' social media monitoring of law enforcement. There are two tasks we need help with:

  • classifying images: sorting images into those that contain officers and those that do not contain officers
  • tagging images: identifying faces in the image and associating them with officers in our roster.

You can see links to the two tasks when logged in this link , shown in the image above.

Task 1: Classify Images

When an image is displayed, look if you see any faces of police officers. If you do, see if you can make out anything about their badge number or name. If you can even see a few letters or numbers, click "Police officers are clearly visible with badge numbers or names". A message will display letting you know that your classification has been saved.

If you don't see any clear faces of police officers in the image with names or badge numbers, just click "No police officers with badge numbers or names are visible" to advance to the next image. Again, a message will display letting you know that your classification has been saved.

Task 2: Identify Officers

In this task, you will map faces each image to an entry in our roster.

An image that has been flagged by volunteers as containing police officers will be displayed. We want you to use the tool to select an officer face in the image, and then use the search box to try to find them in our roster. A preview of the area to be selected will be shown on the left. Open the search tool by clicking "Launch OpenOversight ID search form in new tab".

Once you find the officer, select them and click "Add face tag". Follow this process for each officer in the image.

Once you've added every officer from the image, click "I have tagged all officers I can find identifications for in the image.". Important: only then will your work on the image be saved, so make sure to click this to save your tags.

Checking Your Progress

As you contribute to OpenOversight, you can look at your classifications and tags by going to your profile.

You can also see how you are doing compared to other volunteers by checking out the leaderboard.
